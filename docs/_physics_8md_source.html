6 Physics Factory {#PhysicsFactory}
7 ---------------
8 
9 The physics factory is used to create physics related objects. The most important type of such objects is the _shape_. Here's a list of the most commonly used shapes:
10 
11 | Shape Type | Factory Call |
12 | ---------- | ------------ |
13 | `ShapeType.Box` | `createBox(Vec3(0.5, 0.5, 0.5)) -- 1x1x1 meters => These are half-extends!` |
14 | `ShapeType.Sphere` | `createSphere(1.0) -- 1 meter radius` |
15 | `ShapeType.Capsule` | `createCapsule(Vec3(0.0, 0.0, 0.0), Vec3(0.0, 0.0, 1.0), 1.0) -- start, end, radius => 1 meter height and radius` |
16 | `ShapeType.Cylinder` | `createCylinder(Vec3(0.0, 0.0, 0.0), Vec3(0.0, 0.0, 1.0), 1.0) -- start, end, radius => 1 meter height and radius` |
17 | `ShapeType.Triangle` | `createTriangle(Vec3(0.0, 0.0, 0.0), Vec3(1.0, 0.0, 0.0), Vec3(0.0, 0.0, 1.0)) => vertex0, vertex1, vertex2` |

19 Rigid Body Properties {#RigidBodyProps}
20 ---------------------
21 
22 A rigid body has many properties. Below you will find a list describing each of them with the name they can be found in the `RigidBodyCInfo`. The rigid body itself offers getters and setters to most of these properties, but not all of them. The shape for example can only be set in the `RigidBodyCInfo` that you pass to `createRigidBody`.
23 
24 | Name | Default Value | Description |
25 | ---- | ------------- | ----------- |
26 | `collisionFilterInfo` | `0` | The filter mask for this rigid body. |
27 | `mass` | `0.0` | The mass of the rigid body. |
28 | `shape` | `null` | The shape to use. This must be set to create a valid rigid body. See \ref PhysicsFactory to see how to create shapes. |
29 | `motionType` | `MotionType.Fixed` | The motion type for this igid body. See \ref MotionType for all available types. |
30 | `restitution` | `0.4` | A factor determining how much energy is 'absorbed' upon collision. Be aware that the resitution of all participants in a collision are taken into account. |
31 | `position` | `Vec3(0.0, 0.0, 0.0)` | The world position of the rigid body. |
32 | `rotation` | `Quat() -- Identity quaternion` | The world rotation of this rigid body. |
33 | `friction` | `0.5` | Determines how fast a rigid body loses linear velocity when 'sliding' on another physical entity. |
34 | `linearVelocity` | `Vec3(0.0, 0.0, 0.0)` | The velocity of this rigid body. |
35 | `angularVelocity` | `Vec3(0.0, 0.0, 0.0)` | The angular velocity of this rigid body. |
36 | `linearDamping` | `0.0` | A factor determining how much linear velocity is lost with each simulation step. This basically mimics friction with air. |
37 | `angularDamping` | `0.0` | A factor determining how much angular velocity is lost with each simulation step. |
38 | `gravityFactor` | `1.0` | Determines how much the object is affected by gravity. |
39 | `rollingFrictionMultiplier` | `0.0` | TBD |
40 | `maxLinearVelocity` | `200.0` | Maximum linear speed for this rigid body. |
41 | `maxAngularVelocity` | `200.0` | Maximum angular speed for this rigid body. |
42 | `enableDeactivation` | `true` | TBD |
43 | `timeFactor` | `1.0` | Enables you to use slow motion on a pre-rigid body basis. |
44 | `contactPointCallbackDelay` | `65.535` -- In hex: 0xFFFF | TBD |
45 | `autoRemoveLevel` | `0` | TBD |
46 | `responseModifierFlags` | `0` | TBD |
47 | `numShapeKeysInContactPointProperties` | `0` | TBD |
48 | `isTriggerVolume` | `false` | Determines whether this rigid body is a trigger or not. See \ref TriggerVolumes for more information. |

52 Motion Types {#MotionType}
53 ------------
54 
55 Defined as an enum-like table in Lua. The available values are:
56 
57 ~~~{.lua}
58 MotionType.Dynamic
59 MotionType.SphereInertia
60 MotionType.BoxInertia
61 MotionType.Keyframed
62 MotionType.Fixed
63 MotionType.ThinBoxInertia
64 MotionType.Character
65 ~~~
66 
67 The most important ones are `MotionType.Dynamic`, `MotionType.Fixed`, and `MotionType.Keyframed`.
68 
69 A dynamic rigid body is a fully simulated physics object that will get pushed around according to its mass and the force that is trying to push it.
70 
71 A fixed rigid body, also commonly referred to as a static rigid body, is a physics object that has basically an infinite mass. It cannot be pushed around using forces and it is not simulated using linear velocity. You can use this as environmental geometry which should not move, such as a wall or the ground.
72 
73 A key-framed rigid body is like a fixed rigid body except that it is movable using linear velocity. This is useful for all player controlled entities, such as the paddles in pong.
